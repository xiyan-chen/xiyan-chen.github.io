Which dealing method produces higher card entropy?

Riffle shuffle versus Overhead shuffle

I compared the two most common shuffling methods. Entropy of the shuffling results is calculated based on the probability distributon of shuffled cards using the Shannon entropy equation:
$$ H(X) = - \sum_{i=1}^{n} p_i \log_2 p_i $$

where \( p_i \) is the probability of the \( i^{th} \) outcome. For example, in card shuffling, each possible deck order represents one outcome, and a perfect shuffle makes all outcomes equally likely, maximizing the entropy.

Using 8 cards as an example, 1000000 trials was conducted in total (which is higher than factorial 8). The maximum entropy should be the natural logarithm of the factorial of the number of cards. For each trial/the \(i^{th}\) outcome, both shuffle methods are repeated 6 times. And turns out that the final entropy using overhead shuffle is higher than using riffle shuffle, and both are smaller than the maximum possible entropy.
